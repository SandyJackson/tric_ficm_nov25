A (brief) look ‘under the hood’

How do computers find the right weights?

  • Weights are the numbers we can adjust to make the model fit the data
  • They are random to begin with
  • They try and minimise a ‘loss function’
    • Make the squares smaller!
  • Gradient descent tells them the direction and magnitude to change

Key takeaways

  • \(y = m.x+c\)
  • You can only change 2 things in linear regression
  • You can do an awful lot with this
    • Original POSSUM model has 19 variables
    • 3 ‘weights’ in the final model
    • Around 30 in NELA Risk Calculator
